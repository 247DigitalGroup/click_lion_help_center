                                          How to register a domain and start getting revenue from your traffic?

                                          To register a domain as publisher, please follow these steps:

                                          1. Sign in to your ClickLion account.

                                          2. InPublisher section on the top navigation bar, click to open the domain registration form.

                                          3. There are two steps to register a domain. First you will need to declare your domain, second you are required to authorize your ownership of the domain.

                                            1. Fill your domain in Address field. The domain must include protocols (http, https). For instance, http://apublisher.com is valid while publisher.com is not. The domain must not be authorized by any other users.

                                            2. Turn Sensitive on if you allow sensitive content to be recommended on your website. Learn more about sensitive content in our policies.

                                            3. Click Create to register the domain and move to step 2.

                                            4. Download verification code. It is a HTML file containing a secret code for us to recognize the domain.

                                            5. Upload the file to your domain so we can reach it via http://yourdomain.com/clicklion_code.html where http://yourdomain.com is the domain address you registered.

                                            6. Click Verify after you have done all above steps.

                                          Your domain will have status of unauthorized until you complete verification process (Learn more about domain statuses). You can verify your domain anytime you want.

                                          Understand your domain

                                          Domain is where you can add creatives to put it in your website. It's important for publishers to understand their domains.

                                          States (Statuses)

                                          A domain will likely run through some states. There are totally 4 states that a domain could be:

                                          unauthorized -> reviewing <-> disapproved
                          |
                           -> approved ---> (suspended)

                                          • unauthorized: When you registered your domain, that domain will start with unauthorized state. When the domain is in this state, you cannot do anything except authorizing it.

                                          • reviewing: After you have authorized the domain, its state will be reviewing. In this state, your domain is being reviewed by ClickLion staffs. If it's approved, it's state will be approved, otherwise it's disapproved.

                                          • approved: In this state, your domain is ready to run, you can implement its creatives to start earning revenue.

                                          • disapproved: In this state, you should see a warning in your domain page telling the reason for us to disapproved your domain. You can fix the issue and us if you want us to review your domain one more time.

                                          • suspended: In case your domain interferes with our policies, we will suspend it and obviously its state will be suspended.

                                          If your domain is not in approved state and you still implement its creatives on your website. The creatives will show empty recommendation. See more to understand your creatives.

                                          Subdomains

                                          Right nowm, ClickLion considers subdomains as different domains to the root domain. For example, example.com and us.example.com are considered as 2 separated domains. So if you want a creative to be on many subdomains, it's impossible for now.

                                          Multiple subdomains will be supported in next release.

                                          Add creatives to your domain

                                          To add creatives to your domain, please follow these steps:

                                          1. Sign in to your ClickLion account.

                                          2. In Publisher section on top navigation, click on the domain you wanted to add creatives to. If you don't have any domains there, learn more on how to register a domain.

                                          3. Click on Add Creative then choose which style of creative do you want to add. There are two styles Text and Text & Photo. With Text style, your creative will recommend text only articles that have on title. With Text & Photo, it will serve articles with title and image.

                                          4. After you chose one style, a form for you to add creative will show up. Fill in necessary fields and click Save to add the creative to the domain.

                                          Implement recommendation code on your website

                                          ClickLion recommends articles to your readers by executing a script. That script is loaded and run right after your reader visits your website. To implement that script, please follow these steps:

                                          1. Sign in to your ClickLion account.

                                          2. Make sure you already have an approved domain. If you didn't, learn more on how to register a domain.

                                          3. On Publisher section, click on the domain you are going to implement recommendation code.

                                          4. Click on a creative in the table. If you don't have any creative, learn more on how to create a creative.

                                          5. Look for CODE SNIPPET section, copy the text in grey box. It's the recommendation code for your creative.

                                          6. Copy and paste that script to your website (every page you want CLickLion to recommend articles to readers).

                                          7. Deploy your updated website to make sure your reader will have ClickLion code in your markup.

                                          Customize your creatives

                                          Each creative can be customized in Edit creative page. There are some basic configurations to customize how your creatives display in your website.

                                          General Configs

                                          Config Description Value
                                          Number of articles The number of items displayed in your creative. 1 - 10
                                          Number of internal articles The number of internal article displayed in your creative (less than or equal to number of articles mentioned above). 1 - 10
                                          Number of columns The number of horizontally divided columns in your creative. 1 - 5
                                          Show article source Allow source text (example: clicklion.com) to be shown in each creative's article item. yes/no

                                          Widget Configs

                                          Text Creative

                                          Config Description Value
                                          Show bullets Show a bullet before each creative's article item. yes/no

                                          Manual Configs

                                          Check out the following HTML source for further understanding of how to manually style your creative:

                                          Text Creative

                                          <!-- creative start -->
<div class="cl-wr">
   <div class="cl-ab"><a href="/" target="_blank">Recommended by</a></div>
   <ul class="cl-awoi [cl-bl] [cl-gr-2 cl-gr-3 cl-gr-4 cl-gr-5]">
      <li>
         <a href="http://article.url.goes.here">
            <h4 class="cl-t">Article Title Goes Here</h4>
            <p class="cl-s">Article Source Goes Here</p>
         </a>
      </li>
      <!-- more article item goes here -->
   </ul>
</div>
<!-- creative end -->

                                          Text & Photo Creative

                                          <!-- creative start -->
<div class="cl-wr">
   <div class="cl-ab"><a href="/" target="_blank">Recommended by</a></div>
   <ul class="cl-awi cl-bl">
      <li class="h">
         <a href="http://article.url.goes.here" target="_blank">
            <div style="background-image: url(http://article.image.url.goes.here)" class="cl-i"></div>
            <h4 class="cl-t">Article Title Goes Here</h4>
            <p class="cl-s">Article Source Goes Here</p>
         </a>
      </li>
      <!-- more article item goes here -->
   </ul>
</div>
<!-- creative end -->

                                          Classes Definition

                                          • cl-wr: outmost wrapper for creative

                                          • cl-ab: ClickLion About element, holding the ClickLion homepage link

                                          • cl-awoi: list of articles (without image) - use in Text Creative only

                                          • cl-awi: list of articles (with image) - use in Text & Photo Creative only

                                          • cl-bl (optional): show bullets before each creative's article items

                                          • cl-gr-n (optional): divide creative's article items into n columns

                                          • cl-i: article image class

                                          • cl-t: article title class

                                          • cl-s: article source class

                                          Based on classes definition above, you can write your own CSS and override default creative styles by adding your CSS to Additional CSS field in Edit creative page.

                                          Happy customizing!

                                          Understand your creatives

                                          Creative is one of the most important things to publishers. In this document, we will cover a few key points you need to understand about Creative:

                                          • A creative works only in its domain. There is no way for you to share creatives between domains.

                                          • Considering the term domain, a domain and its subdomains are recognized as separated domains. For example: example.com and us.example.com are considered two different domains by ClickLion.

                                          • A creative might show nothing due to the following errors:

                                            • The creative is not running on its assigned domain

                                            • Its domain has not been approved by ClickLion staffs or has been banned

                                            • The reader activity violates our policies

                                             

                                          • A creative might show irrelevant articles because we have not finished analyzing your websites. The problem should be fixed as long as we do, but if any issue still persists after 48 hours from the time you implemented recommendation code, please contact us for further supports.
